# The Consumerization of B2B: Modern UX for the Yard

Legacy ERPs feel like work. GableLBM should feel like **support**. We are mapping modern B2C expectations into the B2B LBM environment.

## 1. The "Uber" Expectation (Visibility)
- **Old Way:** "Where's the truck?" involves 3 phone calls and a busy signal.
- **New Way:** Real-time map shared with the contractor. "Your studs are 5 minutes away on Flatbed #4."
- **Disruptor:** Reduces "Hot Shot" calls to the counter by 40%.

## 2. The "Amazon" Expectation (Search & Discovery)
- **Old Way:** Searching by cryptic SKU like `248SPF`. 
- **New Way:** Semantic, fuzzy search. "That green flashing for the roof."
- **Disruptor:** Empowers new hires to sell like veterans on day one.

## 3. The "Instagram" Expectation (Visual Proof)
- **Old Way:** Writing "Order Delivered" on a paper slip.
- **New Way:** High-res photos of the drop site attached to the invoice instantly.
- **Disruptor:** Eliminates 90% of damage and "never received" claims.

## 4. The "Apple" Expectation (Simplicity & Haptics)
- **Old Way:** Beeping errors that don't explain why.
- **New Way:** Subtle haptic feedback on handhelds, clear color coding, and "Undo" buttons for every action.
- **Disruptor:** Reduces human error in high-stress morning rushes.

## 5. The "Shopify" Expectation (Dealer Freedom)
- **Old Way:** Changing a price or adding a SKU requires "Batch Updating" or "Nightly Syncs."
- **New Way:** Instant, reactive updates. Change a price at the desk, and the consumer's mobile app updates in milliseconds.
- **Disruptor:** Allows for real-time market-responsive pricing during period of lumber volatility.
