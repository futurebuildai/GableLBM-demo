# Commercial Pricing Strategy: GableLBM Managed Service

**Role**: Managed ERP Implementor & Cloud MSP.
**Target**: Mid-Size Lumber Yards (10-50 Users).
**Value Proposition**: "Modern Performance, Zero IT Headache."

## 1. Upfront Implementation Fees (One-Time)
*Rationale: ERP migrations are 80% data cleaning and human training. Do not undercharge here. This validates commitment.*

### A. The "Quick Start" Package: **$15,000** (For Tier 1)
*   **Data Migration**: Simple CSV Product/Customer import.
*   **Remote Only**: 3 Days of Zoom training.

### B. The "Full Yard" Package: **$35,000+** (For Tier 2/3)
*   **Complex Data**: Historical data cleansing, "Bad SKU" cleanup.
*   **On-Site Go-Live**: 3-5 days on-site during switchover (Critical value add).
*   **Hardware Setup**: Configuring receipt printers, iPads, scanners.

---

## 2. Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) - The 3-Tier Model
*Strategy: "Bucket" pricing encourages owners to give access to everyone (yard guys, drivers), increasing stickiness. Avoid "per-seat" friction.*

### Tier 1: "Starter Yard" (The 5-10 Person Team)
*   **Price**: **$950 / month**
*   **Limit**: Up to 10 Users.
*   **Infrastructure**: Low-Cost Config (~$110 cost).
*   **Target**: Small independent yards, hardware stores.
*   **Psychology**: "Under $1k" is an easy credit card swipe.
*   **Profit**: ~$840/mo.

### Tier 2: "Growth Yard" (The 20-50 Person Team) -> **SWEET SPOT**
*   **Price**: **$2,500 / month**
*   **Limit**: Up to 50 Users.
*   **Infrastructure**: High-Performance Config (4 ACU Aurora, ~$400 cost).
*   **Target**: The typical multi-generation family lumber yard.
*   **Value**: "For less than the cost of one yard guy, you run your whole business."
*   **Profit**: ~$2,100/mo.

### Tier 3: "High Volume" (The 100+ Person Team)
*   **Price**: **$6,000+ / month** (Custom Quote)
*   **Limit**: Unlimited Users / Multiple Locations.
*   **Infrastructure**: Dedicated Cluster (Auto-scaling to max).
*   **Includes**: Priority Support SLA, Dedicated Account Manager.
*   **Target**: Regional chains.
*   **Profit**: High, but labor intensive support.

---

## 3. Financial Outlook (Scenario: Mixed Portfolio)

If you capture:
*   5 "Starter" Clients (@ $950) = $4,750/mo
*   10 "Growth" Clients (@ $2,500) = $25,000/mo
*   1 "High Volume" Client (@ $6,000) = $6,000/mo

**Total MRR**: **$35,750 / month** ($429k/year recurring).
**Infrastructure Cost**: ~$6,000 / month.
**Net Profit**: **~$30,000 / month** (before salaries).

## Strategic Advice
1.  **Don't punish growth**. If a Tier 1 customer grows to 12 users, don't force a jump to $2,500. Offer a "User Pack" ($100/mo for 5 extra users) as a bridge.
2.  **The "Adoption" Argument**: Tell owners, "I want your yard guys using this on their phones so inventory is accurate. That's why I don't charge per seat. I want *more* usage, not less." this resonates with owners tired of "per-seat" license fees for unused accounts.
3.  **120 Users is a Milestone**: At that size, they need Custom Reporting and API Access. That is the upsell to Tier 3.
